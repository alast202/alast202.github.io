Did you know?

The Earth's Surface is 70% water.

Water has three different forms:
Ice (solid), Water(liquid) and Vapour (gas)

The water cycle has been recycling our planets water for millions of years...

This means we drink the same water that the dinosaurs did millions of years ago!

The First Stage of the Water Cycle is Called...

Evaporation

The Water Cycle is powered by the sun.

The sun's heat raises the temperature of our rivers, lakes, and oceans. This causes surface water molecules to change into vapour. This process is called evaporation.

When water changes form and becomes gas (Vapour), it then rises into the atmosphere.This is due to the fact that its molecular structure is less dense than that of the other gases in the Earth's atmosphere, nitrogen and oxygen.

Plants can also produce water vapour as a result of heat, a process known as transpiration.

The Next Stage is Called:

Condensation

As vapour rises higher into the atmosphere, the water molecules begin to cool and change state, this process is called condensation.


When air vapour molecules are cooled, they condense into small water droplets.

Some more facts about the Water Cycle...

In the last Iceage, the ocean levels were 400 feet lower than they were today! This is because so much of the earths water was stored in glaciers and icecaps.

An icecap is a large area (less than 50,000km2) that is covered in ice

Precipication

Precipitation is water vapor or moisture that falls from clouds in the form of rain, sleet, snow, or hail. All liquid or frozen water that forms in the atmosphere and falls to Earth is precipitation.

Precipitation always falls in the form of freshwater, despite mainly coming from the Earth's oceans. This is because salt does not evaporate.

Precipitation plays an important role in this Water Cycle. Without the falling of rain and snow on the land, waterways would quickly dry out and the cycle would not be able to repeat and therefore recycle the water in the Earth's atmosphere.

Run-off

is the name given when the water arrives back at land.

Throughout the water cycle, water accumulation occurs. This is when water is stored in seas, lakes, rivers, streams, and underground through this mechanism.


Water is also absorbed into the ground,the water below the Earth's surface, is responsible for feeding plant life, powering our ecosystems, and keeping the planet alive.

How Climate Change Effects the Water Cycle

Climate change can cause both heavy rain and extreme droughts

Higher Temperatures mean there is more evaporation from the land and sea into the atmosphere.

When air gets warmer, it can hold more water vapour. This can lead to more intense rainstorms.

heavy rainstorms can cause flooding. Alot of the water runs off into rivers and streams, doing little to dampen soil.

This, combined with increased temeratures, increases the risk of drought.
Droughts are a lack in water supply. People rely on water so if a community is having a drought, it can be hard to have enough drinking water. This is especially difficult for thefarmers as they need extra water for their crops and livestock.
